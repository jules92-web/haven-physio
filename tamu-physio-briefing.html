⚠️ Key Considerations for Physio
KNUCKLING prevention
  • Paws must ALWAYS be corrected during walking — never allow to knuckle on hard surfaces as this damages paw tops.
  • Socks/shoes mandatory during all walking sessions and outdoor time.
  • Braces from Durga must be properly fitted and secured. Check fit regularly — adjust if needed.
  • If paw injury occurs, immediate treatment required — damage escalates quickly.
E-collar requirement
  • E-collar required continuously. He removes it if not secured with loops to collar. He will chew paws, wounds, and dressings if unprotected.
  • He has self-harmed before (chewed part of a toe). Vigilance essential — even momentary loss of collar can result in injury.
  • Check collar security multiple times daily.
Behaviour & handling
  • Aggressive in cage at times. Selective about who feeds him (dominance). May not accept food from unfamiliar staff.
  • Requires consistent handlers. Build trust and establish routine before introducing new care providers.
  • Do not force interactions — allow him to approach on his own terms.
Bladder & bowel management
  • Bladder: Even if he passes small amounts spontaneously, full emptying requires manual expression with pressure.
  • Constipation: Manage with pumpkin, psyllium husk, and Cremaffin. Can defecate with assistance.
  • Monitor urine colour — should be normal. If dark or concentrated, increase water intake.
Surfaces & environment
  • Non-slippery surfaces only. Grass strongly preferred — provides grip and natural movement feedback.
  • Avoid tile, cement, or vinyl flooring during sessions.
  • Ensure consistent walking routes — familiarity aids his orientation and confidence.
Hydrotherapy notes
  • Pool available — schedule 1–2 sessions weekly if possible.
  • Pool water must be clean and regularly maintained.
  • Monitor for exhaustion during and after sessions. Limit to comfortable duration.
  • Ensure safe entry and exit — may need assistance with positioning.
